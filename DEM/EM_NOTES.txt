EM calculation (quick flare approximation)

Definitions
- DEM proxy (from weighted AIA response): cm^-5 per pixel at a representative logT
- Column EM: cm^-5 = integral of DEM(T) over temperature
- Volume EM per pixel: cm^-3 pixel^-1 = column EM * pixel area

Conceptual picture
- The weighted AIA method produces a single-temperature DEM proxy, not a full DEM(T).
- Treat that proxy as a column quantity (cm^-5) tied to a representative flare temperature.
- EM is obtained by assuming a temperature width and integrating over that range.
- Volume EM per pixel is a geometric conversion from column EM using pixel area.

Flare-bin approximation
- Temperature range: log10(T/K) = 6.6 to 7.2 (T >= 10^6.6 K)
- Bin width: dlogT = 0.1
- Representative temperature: T = 10 MK (1.0e7 K)
- DeltaT = ln(10) * T * dlogT
- Column EM = DEM * DeltaT

Volume EM per pixel
- Pixel area uses arcsec per pixel and 1 AU distance
- A_pix = (arcsec_per_pix * AU_cm * arcsec_to_rad)^2
- EM_vol,pix = EM_col * A_pix

Workflow summary
1) Build DEM proxy maps using fixed weights, EXPTIME, and response normalization.
2) Convert DEM proxy to column EM with DeltaT (flare-bin approximation).
3) Optionally convert column EM to volume EM per pixel using pixel area.

We will get the precalculated temperature response functions at https://hesperia.gsfc.nasa.gov/ssw/sdo/aia/response/
along with the emissivity tables.
